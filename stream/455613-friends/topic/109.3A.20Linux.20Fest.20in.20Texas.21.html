Ron Waldon-Howe (Oct 30 2025 at 02:49):

Listening to this now, finally
RE: GNOME and extensions:

  • I've attempted to write an extension in the past, and GJS is effectively JavaScript as a frontend to C/C++, to the degree that it's extremely easy to make mistakes that will take down the entire desktop session
  • vanilla GNOME is very polished, and as long as your workflow fits the GNOME vision/philosophy, it's the most polished experience you can have on Linux
  • the very moment you want to diverge even the slightest bit from that upstream vision, you should NOT reach for extensions (brittle, often poor quality, prone to disappear after each major GNOME update), rather switch to a different desktop environment or different Wayland compositor
  • this is all personal opinion of course :)
