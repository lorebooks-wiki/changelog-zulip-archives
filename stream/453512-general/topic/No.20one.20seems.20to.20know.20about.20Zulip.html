James Thurley (Sep 25 2024 at 16:09):

It's like Slack, except:

In Slack channels are just free-flowing conversations, often with multiple conversations overlapping, questions getting lost in the noise, sometimes threads being used and sometimes not, sometimes conversations jumping back out of threads into the main channel, etc.

In Zulip you also have channels, but channels contain distinct "topics" which are kind of like mini, dynamic, almost transient, channels in Slack. Every conversation in the channel happens in it's own topic. It makes it easier to handle having many parallel distinct conversations, not limited by the number of channels, compared to Slack.

James Thurley (Sep 25 2024 at 16:13):

As an example, in my work Slack we have a channel called platform-technical for technical conversations about our platform.

But at some point people are deep in technical discussion, not using threads (because threads in Slack are a bit half-arsed), and you want to start another conversation. So what do you do? Well, we create a new channel called platform-technical-2 and use that when platform-technical is busy. Eventually we also ended up with platform-technical-3 for the same reason.

In Zulip, you can just have the one platform-technical channel, because all those conversations are distinct topics within the channel.

Jerod Santo (May 09 2025 at 14:11):

@Alya Abbott

On URLS

I don't find URLs like this very readable:

/#narrow/channel/457248-applenerds/topic/Is.20there.20any.20way.20to.20fine.20tune.20find.20my.3F/with/501702223

I think you're trying to embed too much information in it. This is much better (imho):

/#/channel/applenerds/topic/501702223

(I'm not sure what's up with the #narrow part, but I'm sure there's a story there...)

Or you could go one step further and give channels primacy by assuming if there isn't a special prefix (such as /dm/) in the URL then it's a channel. Then it could be:

/#/applenerds/topic/501702223

And linking to a message could be:

/#/applenerds/topic/501702223/message/8675309

You could even establish a default pattern where it's always: /:channel/:topic/:message. Then deep-linking to a specific Zulip message would be as clean as:

/#/applenerds/501702223/8675309

I'm sure there's all kinds of technical reasons why making these kind of changes throughout the app would be difficult, and I'm not even suggesting that it's worth your while. I just know pretty URLs when I see them (GitHub is a great example of this) and Zulip lacks in that (admittedly narrow) category.

On emoji

The emoji picker on desktop is fine, but the mobile one is super slow to use. What's lacking from both is some kind of "recently used" or "frequently used" that is customized to you. Another way of tackling that same need is by letting people sticky their favorite/frequent emoji to the top/front.

An example: I'm a :green_heart: guy. I pretty much never use the red one (ok one person gets the red one, but she's not here :wink:). The red one may be popular, but I have no use for it in this context, so I have to go fetch the green one each time.

Each of us probably have a set of emoji that we use often enough that we'd like some more advanced features to improve the experience.
